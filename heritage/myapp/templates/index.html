Sacred Heritage Sites

Khajuraho Temples

Built between 950-1050 CE by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are famous for their nagara-style architectural symbolism and intricate sculptures.

Madhya Pradesh

Konark Sun Temple

A 13th-century temple dedicated to the Sun God, shaped like a massive chariot with intricate stone carvings and architectural excellence.

Odisha

Hampi Temple Complex

The capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, featuring stunning temple architecture and ruins from the 14th-16th centuries.

Karnataka

Meenakshi Temple

A historic temple dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi, known for its 14 magnificent towers and 33,000 sculptures.

Tamil Nadu

Kedarnath Temple

An ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, situated in the Himalayan ranges at an altitude of 3,583 meters.

Uttarakhand

Somnath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, rebuilt several times in the Chalukya style of temple architecture.

Gujarat

About India's Sacred Heritage

Historical Significance

India's temples and monuments represent over 2000 years of rich cultural and architectural heritage, showcasing the evolution of various architectural styles and spiritual traditions.

These sacred sites are protected under the Archaeological Survey of India and many are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Architectural Styles

  • Nagara Architecture of North India
  • Dravidian Architecture of South India
  • Vesara Style - A fusion of North and South
  • Regional variations and local influences
